Tenses

By Isaac Kuri, Ignacio Escalera, Manuel Montejo
Past Perfect
  • Affirmative: I had been in the UPY for a long time, however, I’m still not used to the schedules.
  • Negative: They hadn’t study for the final english test, therefore, they failed it.
  • Interrogative: Had the teacher been disapproving the student’s behaviour, thus, taking the chance to give them an advice?
Present Tenses
  • Continuous: I am stuying some programing lenguages right now, in other words, I am not leaving the house for the moment.
  • Perfect: I have spent some hours on my digital system homework,In contrast, many of my classmates have not even try yet.
  • Perfect Continuous: I have been reading my coding book call “clean code” for hours, furthermore, I have tried to make some “hello world” for practice.
Future (Going to)
  • Affirmative: I’m going to Canada next year, in addition to this, I'm also going to have a great time doing so.
  • Negative: The teachers are not going to give classes this friday, nevertheless, the quizes are still available until saturday.
  • Interrogative: Are they the ones that are going to compete in the water rocket contest? Otherwise I’m not going to compete.
